# Olympic Power Structure

This project explores the relationship between countries’ characteristics and their performance in the Olympic Games.

The analysis is divided into four main steps, each corresponding to a notebook.

---

##  1. Data Preparation

In this first step, multiple datasets (Olympic results, GDP, and country-level indicators) are collected, cleaned, and merged.

Main operations:

* Handling missing values and inconsistencies
* Standardizing country names and time formats
* Aggregating data at country-year level
* Creating derived variables (e.g., total medals)

---

## 2. Exploratory Analysis

We perform an initial exploration of the dataset to understand general patterns.

Key analyses:

* Distribution of medals across countries and time
* Evolution of Olympic participation
* Identification of dominant countries
* Preliminary visualizations (heatmaps, trends)

---

## 3. GDP and Olympic Success

This section investigates the relationship between economic power and Olympic performance.

Main points:

* Correlation between GDP and number of medals
* Comparison between countries with similar economic levels
* Identification of overperforming and underperforming countries

---

## 4. Specialization Analysis

This step focuses on countries’ specialization in specific sports.

Key analyses:

* Medal distribution across sports for each country
* Identification of top sports per country
* Heatmap visualizations to highlight specialization patterns

---
## 5. Additional Olympic Dynamics

In this section, we extend the analysis by exploring additional dynamics of Olympic performance.

* Summer vs Winter Olympics: Do countries perform similarly across the two Olympic seasons, or do they specialize?
* Evolution over time: How has Olympic performance changed across different editions of the Games?
* Home advantage: Do host countries perform better when they organize the Olympic Games?
